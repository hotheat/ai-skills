# Survey Mode

Use this mode for survey, review, taxonomy, landscape, roadmap, corpus, and systematic mapping papers.

## Goal

Do not only summarize the literature. Produce a judgement about what the paper changes in the reader's view of the field, and show the evidence strength behind that judgement.

## Required Argument Chain

1. Organizing question: what field-level confusion, fragmentation, or blind spot does the paper reorganize?
2. Scope boundary: covered domains, time span, artifact types, sources, and explicit exclusions.
3. Inclusion method: source streams, search terms if stated, screening, inclusion/exclusion, deduplication, coding, audit, snapshot date.
4. Taxonomy: axes/layers, hierarchy, layer relationships, neighboring concepts, likely misclassifications.
5. Representative matrix: work/project x category x task domain x year x evidence type x artifact availability.
6. Evidence ladder: which claims are backed by systematic tables, which are author synthesis, and which are inference.
7. Operational verdict: what should a researcher or engineer believe after reading this survey?
8. Structural bottlenecks: field gaps that explain why the problem remains unsolved.
9. What would change the verdict: missing experiments, benchmarks, release artifacts, or field coverage that would materially alter the conclusion.

## Required Visual Modules

- Verdict Board: thesis, evidence grade, largest blind spot, structural bottleneck, action.
- Corpus Pipeline: source streams -> candidates -> inclusion/exclusion -> coding -> taxonomy -> audit.
- Taxonomy Map: hierarchy or axes with layer relations.
- Coverage Matrix: categories vs domains/systems/evidence type.
- Claim-Evidence Ledger: claim, source, evidence level, risk.
- Gap Cards: 3-5 structural gaps, each with evidence and consequence.

## Conclusion Shape

Use this order:

1. Verdict.
2. Evidence supporting the verdict.
3. Structural bottlenecks or blind spots.
4. What evidence would change the verdict.
5. Research actions and engineering actions.

Avoid vague endings such as "more work is needed." Name the exact missing test, corpus slice, coding protocol, benchmark, or deployment evidence.
